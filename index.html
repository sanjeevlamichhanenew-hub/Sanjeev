About Me

Academic Background & Research Interests

I believe engineering is as much an art of observation as it is a science of calculation. By stripping away noise to find core principles, I find my greatest momentum in brainstorming and problem-solving. I thrive in the "messiness" of new challenges, always seeking the "why" before the "how," valuing the quiet discipline of research and the clarity of a well-understood problem.

I'm actively seeking PhD opportunities worldwide to further explore my interests in manufacturing and advanced materials.
